**Implementation Plan for Quantyra-Teaching Repository**

**Goal**
The goal of this implementation plan is to refine and validate the Quantyra-Teaching repository, ensuring it remains grounded in its current scope and artifacts.

**Constraints**
The implementation will be restricted to the current repository and its existing artifacts.

**Required Artifacts**

* Benchmark packets
* Task packets
* Run folders
* Evaluator worksheets
* Score summaries
* Comparison memos
* Checkpoint updates

**Dependencies**

* The Quantyra-Teaching repository must be set up to track and store all required artifacts.
* The pilot setup must confirm the following repo-local details:
	+ Benchmark packet review/refinement process
	+ Task packet and run folder organization
	+ Evaluator worksheet and score summary templates
	+ Comparison memo and checkpoint update procedures

**Validation Checkpoints**

* Initial review of benchmark packets and task packets to ensure alignment with repository scope.
* Regular review and refinement of benchmark packets to ensure accuracy and relevance.
* Periodic review of score summaries and comparison memos to ensure consistency and fairness.
* Completion of checkpoint updates to document lessons learned and process improvements.

**Risks**

* Inadequate refinement of benchmark packets may lead to biased or irrelevant evaluations.
* Failure to track and store required artifacts may compromise the integrity of the repository.

**Fallback Paths**

* In the event of inadequate refinement of benchmark packets, alternative approaches will be explored and implemented.
* In the event of failure to track and store required artifacts, alternative storage solutions will be identified and implemented.

**Closeout Scorecard**

* Finalized benchmark packets
* Finalized task packets
* Completed score summaries
* Completed comparison memos
* Completed checkpoint updates

**Genuine Teaching vs. Prompt Overfitting**

* Genuine teaching will be defined as the ability of the repository to provide accurate and relevant evaluations, without overreliance on specific prompts or artifacts.
* Prompt overfitting will be identified as the tendency to rely too heavily on specific prompts or artifacts, compromising the integrity and generalizability of the repository.

**Pilot Evaluation**

* The pilot will assess the effectiveness of the repository in tracking and storing required artifacts.
* The pilot will evaluate the refinement of benchmark packets and the accuracy of score summaries.
* The pilot will identify areas for improvement and provide recommendations for repository refinement.

**Bias/Confound Controls**

* The repository will track and store data on evaluator demographics and biases.
* The repository will implement randomization and stratification procedures to control for confounds.
* The repository will regularly review and refine benchmark packets to ensure they are free from bias and confounds.

**Lessons Learned**

* Lessons learned from the pilot will be fed back into the refinement of benchmark packets, ensuring that the repository remains grounded in its current scope and artifacts.
* Regular review and refinement of benchmark packets will ensure that the repository remains relevant and accurate.
