# Internal Manuscript Review Checklist V1

## Purpose
Provide a consistent internal review checklist for the current manuscript packet after hosted human `L2` integration.

## How to use
- Review the packet in the order listed in `docs/evidence/manuscript-packet-v1.md`.
- Record comments against the sections below.
- Treat this checklist as review scaffolding for final editorial cleanup, not as a substitute for distinct-evaluator scoring.

## 1. Claim discipline

- Does the draft clearly separate:
  - hosted primary evidence
  - local secondary resilience evidence
  - completed hosted human `L2` support on the hosted slice
- Does any section overclaim local evidence as replacement-quality parity?
- Does any section imply evaluator independence is already resolved?
- Are the main claims in the draft aligned with `docs/evidence/manuscript-claim-matrix-v1.md`?

## 2. Results integrity

- Are the hosted results described consistently with `BP001` through `BP004`?
- Are the local results described consistently with `BP007B` and `BP008`?
- Is the BP006 architecture discussion consistent with the retained pair synthesis?
- Do any prose summaries drift beyond the retained score summaries or synthesis memos?

## 3. Limits and caveats

- Is the remaining `L0/L1` versus numeric-transcription limitation visible enough?
- Are proxy-materialized workflow limits stated clearly enough?
- Are local hardware/runtime constraints represented honestly?
- Does the current limits language feel appropriately conservative for publication preparation?

## 4. Figures and tables

- Do Table 1 through Table 4 match the current retained evidence?
- Do the figure captions match the current figure exports?
- Does Figure 2 clearly communicate hosted-primary versus local-resilience posture?
- Does Figure 3 communicate BP006 architecture outcomes without overstating completeness?

## 5. Publication posture

- Is the current publication position clear:
  - hosted results are the paper spine
  - local results are bounded resilience evidence
- Does the packet support internal paper planning and disclosure review?
- Is there any wording that should be softened before broader circulation?
- Is the remaining blocker framed narrowly as numeric transcription verification and final packaging?

## 6. Editorial quality

- Is the abstract sharp enough on the two-layer evidence posture?
- Are the section transitions clear?
- Are benchmark-family references specific enough for a technical reader?
- Are there any sections that still read like internal notes rather than manuscript prose?

## Recommended output format

Use simple notes under:
- strengths
- concerns
- must-fix items before broader circulation
- optional improvements after `L2`
- optional improvements after final packaging
