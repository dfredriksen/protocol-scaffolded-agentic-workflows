# Authorship And Contribution Note V1

Date: 2026-03-15

## Purpose

Record the current authorship posture and contribution rule for the manuscript while review work is still underway.

## Current Role Framing

- Chief Research Officer: program direction, standards, publication posture, and strategic research oversight
- VP of Research: methodology design, benchmark execution, packet assembly, and manuscript development

## Review Contribution Rule

Human `L2` review alone does not automatically justify authorship.

It supports:

- reviewer credit
- acknowledgments
- reviewer metadata in the internal evidence chain

It does not automatically support:

- manuscript authorship

## When Authorship Becomes Plausible

Authorship may be appropriate if a reviewer contributes substantially beyond review, for example by:

- materially shaping the study design
- materially changing the evaluation methodology
- materially influencing the interpretation of results
- drafting or rewriting substantial portions of the manuscript
- taking public responsibility for the paper’s claims

## Current Recommendation

- Distinct reviewers who perform only `L2` should be credited as reviewers, not authors by default.
- If the VP of Science or another internal reviewer materially shapes the paper beyond `L2`, revisit authorship explicitly before submission.

## Submission Note

This note should be revisited immediately before submission so the authorship line reflects actual scholarly contribution rather than title alone.
