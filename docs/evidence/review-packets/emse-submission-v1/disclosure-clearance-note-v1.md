## Purpose

Record the current disclosure posture for the Teaching manuscript so the submission packet has an explicit disclosure-clearance surface rather than relying only on scattered patentability notes.

## Current recommendation

Current recommendation: `clear with carve-outs`

## Reasoning

The current manuscript is framed as an empirical software-engineering study of protocol-mediated uplift in agent workflows. That framing is narrower than the broad invention-shaped language identified as risky in the patentability position note. The paper does not need to claim that the broad concept of teaching agents with protocols is novel. It can instead report the current benchmarked empirical findings conservatively.

At the same time, the repo still carries an active patentability-scope pass. The strongest possible invention candidate remains narrower than the paper's general framing and appears to center on the integrated lesson-to-protocol conversion, control structure, and measured uplift methodology. That means the paper should remain careful about novelty language and should avoid broad invention-sounding claims that are not necessary for publication.

## Clearance decision

The current packet may proceed toward journal submission if the following carve-outs are preserved:

- do not claim that the broad concept of reusable agent protocol teaching is established as novel
- do not collapse the paper's empirical result into a patentability assertion
- preserve the distinction between the paper's hosted primary claim and any narrower possible invention claim
- keep the paper's current conservative construct language around `teaching`

## Remaining disclosure dependency

This note does not replace counsel review. If the organization decides to pursue patent filing before or alongside publication, the narrower claim-shape memo and targeted prior-art review should still be completed.
