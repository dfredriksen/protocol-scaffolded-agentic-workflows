# Journal Venue Memo v1

Date: 2026-03-15  
Scope: journal-first venue analysis after external readiness review

## Purpose

This memo selects the most plausible journal family for the current manuscript and explains the tradeoffs. The current instruction is explicit: the program is not pursuing conference submission.

## Current Manuscript Shape

After scope narrowing, the paper is best understood as:

- an empirical software-engineering study of protocolized agent workflows
- centered on repeated within-model uplift under reusable protocol artifacts
- with local/open-weight results as bounded resilience evidence

That profile favors software-engineering journals over HCI or classic multiagent venues.

## Venue Ranking

### 1. Empirical Software Engineering

Best fit.

Why:

- the paper is fundamentally empirical
- the main contribution is methodological and engineering-oriented
- the evidence is organized around workflow discipline, evaluation protocol, and replicable benchmark structure
- the hosted/local split reads naturally as an engineering evidence hierarchy

What would still need strengthening:

- fuller methods transparency
- clearer run accounting
- `L2` integration
- stronger external-paper prose instead of internal packet language

### 2. IEEE Software

Good pragmatic fit if the manuscript is made more practice-forward.

Why:

- the work speaks to engineering practice around agent workflows
- the protocol and review-loop framing can be made accessible to practitioners

Main tradeoff:

- this route may require more emphasis on operational lessons and deployment practice than on benchmark nuance

### 3. IEEE Intelligent Systems

Reasonable secondary option.

Why:

- the paper still concerns agentic systems and evaluation patterns
- BP006 and the reviewer-architecture work fit better here than in a purely software-methods venue

Main tradeoff:

- the strongest part of the manuscript is still engineering-methodological rather than general AI-systems novelty

### 4. ACM TOSEM

Conceptual fit, but current manuscript is not ready for this bar.

Why:

- the work is related to software engineering and methodology

Why not primary now:

- current transparency and validation posture are not yet strong enough
- the paper still reads more like a disciplined empirical packet than a mature archival methods paper

### 5. AI & Society

Not recommended for the current framing.

Why:

- the manuscript is not centered on societal or human-centered implications
- the strongest contribution is engineering workflow evidence, not social analysis

## Venue Family Conclusion

Primary target family:

- software-engineering journals

Preferred order:

1. `Empirical Software Engineering`
2. `IEEE Software`
3. `IEEE Intelligent Systems`

Stretch target later:

- `TOSEM`

## What The Venue Decision Implies

If `Empirical Software Engineering` is the target, the paper should:

- foreground empirical design and reporting transparency
- reduce internal program language
- present BP006 as secondary analysis rather than a parallel thesis
- keep local evidence clearly bounded

If `IEEE Software` becomes the target, the paper should:

- become more practice-oriented
- compress benchmark detail
- foreground operational takeaways for engineering teams

## Current Recommendation

Proceed toward `Empirical Software Engineering` as the primary journal target unless later editorial revision pushes the manuscript toward a substantially more practitioner-focused article.
